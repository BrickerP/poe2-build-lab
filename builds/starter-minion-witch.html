Needs in-game testing · patch 0.5.2 Early Access · reviewed 2026-06-16

A minion-oriented Witch starter where summoned bodies absorb pressure and deal damage while the player learns enemy attacks. Beginner-friendly, but minion scaling and Spirit reservation need attention.

Is this build for me?

  • New players who want extra bodies between them and danger while learning.
  • Players who prefer commanding minions over precise aiming.
  • Players who can tolerate slightly more complex scaling (Spirit, minion stats).

What skills do I use?

Raise Zombie main

Your main army skill. Keep zombie count stable before chasing luxury damage supports.

  • Minion Pact I — Core minion damage scaling; Intelligence-weighted.
  • Last Gasp — Helps minions survive longer between resummons.

Frost Wall utility

Blocks enemy paths while minions fight — cheap defensive utility that does not add Spirit reservation.

Blink movement

Short-range escape. Minions do not make you invincible — you still dodge.

Can I equip my gems?

Deficit policy: warn. Required (gems): Str 0 / Dex 0 / Int 100 · Planned loadout: Str 0 / Dex 0 / Int 120.

  • Raise Zombie and linked supports are Intelligence-weighted (100 Int at level 1 from community gem data).
  • Keep Intelligence above your highest gem requirement; the amulet often supplies the buffer.

Do I have enough Spirit?

Required: 0 · Planned: 0

Reservation sources:

  • Weapon (sceptre with Spirit): 0
  • Amulet (Spirit roll): 0
  • Body armour (Spirit roll): 0
  • Minions reserve Spirit. Exact reservation per zombie depends on gem level and is not in our community extract — enter your in-game total in the attribute checker.
  • Do not add persistent minions or aura supports until you know your Spirit capacity.
  • Gear that grants Spirit is part of your reservation budget; swapping it can desummon minions.

What breaks if I swap gear?

amulet: If your amulet supplies Spirit or the Intelligence your minion gems need, swapping it without replacing that Spirit/Intelligence can desummon minions or disable a companion. Breaks: Primary minion skill, Secondary minion or companion
weapon: A weapon that grants Spirit is part of your reservation budget; swapping to a non-Spirit weapon may leave you unable to sustain all minions. Breaks: Secondary minion or companion

How do I not die?

Open defense diagnostic for this build

Campaign targets

  • Keep elemental resistances as high as the campaign allows for your level.
  • Keep your own life or energy shield current — do not hide behind minions alone.
  • Maintain enough minion life that you are not resummoning every pack.

Early maps targets

  • Cap elemental resistances on your character.
  • Have movement speed on boots and Blink for repositioning.
  • Minion survivability stable under map monster damage.
  • Recovery: Personal life/ES regeneration or flasks plus minion leech/regen where available.
  • Movement: Movement speed on boots plus Blink for escapes.
  • Chaos resistance: Chaos resistance is a bonus during campaign, not a priority.

Map mods to avoid

  • Monsters deal extra damage / player takes increased damage (minions and you both suffer).
  • Less recovery — harder to sustain through mistakes.
  • Monsters have increased attack/cast speed before your defenses are capped.

What can go wrong?

Failure modes

  • Minions die repeatedly if their survivability and your Spirit setup are neglected.
  • Minion scaling is patch-sensitive; a balance change can shift power noticeably.
  • Player can become too passive and forget personal defenses.

Risk flags

  • not-in-game-tested
  • patch-sensitive
  • minion-balance-sensitive
