Starter Minion / Companion Witch

Patch: 0.5.2 Early AccessBudget: lowComplexity: mediumGear dependency: mediumImport file not yet available
Draft card. This archetype is research-backed but not yet in-game verified, so it is not indexed for search. Exact gem, base, and passive ids are still being confirmed against verified data before this card is published.

A minion-oriented Witch starter where summoned bodies absorb pressure and deal damage while the player learns enemy attacks. Beginner-friendly, but minion scaling and Spirit reservation need attention.

Who should play this

  • New players who want extra bodies between them and danger while learning.
  • Players who prefer commanding minions over precise aiming.
  • Players who can tolerate slightly more complex scaling (Spirit, minion stats).

Skills & supports

Primary minion skill main

Keep your minion count stable before chasing raw minion damage.

  • Minion damage support — Core damage scaling for the army.
  • Minion survivability support — Fewer resummons means smoother clear.

Secondary minion or companion secondary

A tankier body or utility companion for boss fights.

Movement / escape skill movement

You still need to dodge; minions do not make you invincible.

Passive milestones

Early campaignTake early minion damage and minion life clusters near the Witch start.
Mid campaignPick up Spirit / reservation efficiency and personal life nodes.
Late campaignBalance minion survivability with your own defensive masteries before pure damage.

Gear-swap warnings

amulet: If your amulet supplies Spirit or the Intelligence your minion gems need, swapping it without replacing that Spirit/Intelligence can desummon minions or disable a companion. Breaks: Primary minion skill, Secondary minion or companion
weapon: A weapon that grants Spirit is part of your reservation budget; swapping to a non-Spirit weapon may leave you unable to sustain all minions. Breaks: Secondary minion or companion

Defensive checklist

Campaign targets

  • Keep elemental resistances as high as the campaign allows for your level.
  • Keep your own life or energy shield current; do not hide behind minions alone.

Early maps targets

  • Cap elemental resistances.
  • Maintain minion survivability so you are not constantly resummoning under pressure.
  • Recovery: Plan a personal recovery layer plus minion regeneration/leech where available.
  • Movement: Movement speed on boots plus a movement/escape skill.
  • Chaos resistance: Chaos resistance is a bonus during campaign, not a campaign priority.

Known risks & failure modes

Failure modes

  • Minions die repeatedly if their survivability and your Spirit setup are neglected.
  • Minion scaling is patch-sensitive; a balance change can shift power noticeably.
  • Player can become too passive and forget personal defenses.

Risk flags

  • not-in-game-tested
  • patch-sensitive
  • minion-balance-sensitive
